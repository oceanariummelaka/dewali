Diwali
Festival of Lights


Diwali is a festival of light that celebrates the triumph of good light over evil darkness in South Asian cultures and is celebrated over five days in parts of India.
Diwali takes place on the darkest night of the Hindu lunisolar calendar, during which Sikhs, Hindus, Jains and some Buddhists light diyas to celebrate the victory of light. Around this time of year, Sikhs also celebrate Bandi Chhor Divas, commemorating the release of Guru Hargobind and 52 imprisoned Hindu Kings in the early 1600s, coinciding with Diwali.
